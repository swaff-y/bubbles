SCUBA Diving and Pressure

Most of diving is about dealing with pressure.

Pressure is so important there are three different laws that pertain to diving.

  • Boyle's Law
  • Henry's Law
  • Dalton's Law

What is the most important law in SCUBA diving?

Boyle's Law

For an ideal gas pressure and volume are inversely proportional at constant temperature

What does that even mean? WTF. Any guess as to what that entails?

Dalton's Law

The total pressure of a gas is equal to the sum of the partial pressures of its components.

  • +- 30m Nitrogen Narcosis / nitrox
  • +- 66m Oxygen Toxicity / trimix
  • > 100m deep water tremors
Henry's Law

The concentration of a gas dissolved in a liquid is proportional to its partial pressure above the liquid.

  • Diffusion
  • Bends
